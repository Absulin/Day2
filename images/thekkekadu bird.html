About these results

Ernakulam is a vibrant city in Kerala, India, that offers a blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and modern amenities. Whether you want to explore the historic Hill Palace Museum, admire the art and technology at the Madhavan Nayar Foundation, or visit the birthplace of the Hindu philosopher Adi Shankara at Kaladi, Ernakulam has something for everyone. You can also enjoy the scenic views of the Periyar River, the lush greenery of the Thattekkad Bird Sanctuary, or the serene atmosphere of the Malayatoor Church. Ernakulam is a great base to explore the nearby Kochi, the commercial and cultural hub of Kerala, where you can experience the colonial charm, the cosmopolitan lifestyle, and the delicious cuisine of the region.

vizag84
February 3, 2019
I write this review seated inside the Changamuzha Park, as an outcome of the immediate inspiration from my sunset stroll inside this lush green haven at Edapally. The park nested between Edapally and Palarivattom, both counted among the busiest intersections in Kochi, offers an ambience of stark contrast with varieties of trees offering both shade and the much needed oxygen to the city dwellers. The park with 2 entrances, a playarea for kids and a small auditorium with a ground for audiences has an amazing walkway around its edges. The park, named after the legendary poet also holds in its list of attractions an IN-172 fighter jet from the relics of India's proud war history in Kargil. Despite having visited many times, today the park presented an altogether different cultural character of the crowd in my hometown, after a morning which witnessed perhaps the most devastating hartal in recent times. The park was abuzz with activities - light music from malayalam's abundance being sung on stage to full crowds, by a host of talented musicians. Denizens across the spectrum of age, from kids in their parents laps to octagenarians find purpose at the park, some briskly strolling, kids playing, some engaged in carroms in the small rooms inside the park, gentlemen playing chess seated on the benches along the edge of the park with quite a few onlookers, while a lot others sway to the melodious music being sung. I resisted taking a picture of a particularly interesting battle of chess between a young chap all of 10 and an old gentleman with nothing more than half a century separating them, for it would have been obtruse at a place where everyone's walking at their own pace. The park hosts a gamut of activities in its evenings, from Kathakali, visual arts, drama, movie shows, classical music, light music, so often that it fits to be adjudged the cultural capital of Edapally in its capacity, for it brings together people from all walks of life. Fabulous is the energy that this small space houses, so familiar yet so very unique every time you visit. For anyone thats passing by Edapally around 6 and 8 in the evening, may want to drop in to blend in for oneself. Absolutely Recommended !!!!
